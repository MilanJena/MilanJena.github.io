Biography

Dr. Milan Kumar Jena is a computational chemist and materials scientist with over six years of experience at the interface of computational chemistry, quantum transport and machine learning. His research focuses on developing data‑driven frameworks for understanding and predicting charge transport at the atomic and molecular scale, with particular emphasis on next‑generation sequencing and biosensing technologies.

During his PhD at IIT Indore, he integrated first‑principles quantum transport calculations with machine learning to address key challenges in DNA sequencing and molecular identification, using solid‑state nanopores and nanogaps to analyze transverse electron tunneling signals. He developed supervised and unsupervised ML frameworks to classify nucleotides, accounting for electrode–molecule coupling, hydration effects and signal variability. His work highlighted the synergy between first‑principles methods and data‑driven approaches for non‑enzymatic, label‑free molecular sensing.

Currently, as a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Warwick, Dr. Jena is developing physics‑guided machine learning models to accelerate quantum transport calculations and to predict conductance in metallic nanowires. He collaborates with national and international teams on metal nanoclusters and perovskite materials, contributing expertise in electronic structure analysis, time‑dependent DFT and free‑energy calculations.
